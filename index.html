Bojun Hu, Ph.D.

Group Therapy, Relational Experiential Therapy, Trauma and Attachment Therapy, Creative Embodiment

Healing requires that the body have new experiences, not merely new insights.
-- Pat Ogden

At Bojun Hu PLLC, therapy is understood as a living process—one that unfolds through relationship, embodiment, and presence. Rather than focusing solely on insight or symptom reduction, we work with what emerges in the here and now: emotions, bodily sensations, relational patterns, and moments of contact. Healing happens through experience, not in isolation, but in connection.

How We Work Together

We integrate specialized modalities to support depth-oriented, relational healing. Our work attends not only to what you think and feel, but also to how your body responds, how you relate to others, and how meaning emerges through lived experience. Therapy here is collaborative, experiential, and paced with care—inviting curiosity, honesty, and compassion for the ways you have learned to survive and adapt.

Specializations

Specialization How It Helps you
Relational Experimental Therapy Understanding how your past shapes your present while building more secure, authentic connections by moving from knowing to feeling through present-moment emotional and relational experience.
Trauma and Attachment Therapy Working with the nervous system and relational patterns shaped by early attachment and trauma, gently expanding capacity for safety, connection, and self-regulation.
Creative Embodiment Reconnecting with bodily wisdom through movement and creative expression, allowing tension to be expressed relationally and imaginatively, and developing a more fluent bodily language for experience.
Group Therapy Practicing being with yourself while being with others—learning co-regulation, embodied presence, and relational awareness through live interpersonal experience in a facilitated group setting.

When you step into our process, you are invited into a space where growth happens through relationship. Together, we discover what supports your capacity to stay present, to feel more fully, and to engage with yourself and others in ways that feel more alive, grounded, and meaningful.
